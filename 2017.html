NATIONAL ACADEMY OF MEDICINE (NAM)

The National Academy of Medicine (NAM), established in 1970 as the Institute of Medicine, is one of the three academies that make up the National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine (NASEM) in the United States. It serves as a national resource for independent, scientifically informed analysis and recommendations on health and medical issues.

REASON FOR ELECTION

Fei-Fei Li was elected to NAM in recognition of her pioneering work in artificial intelligence and computer vision, which has profound implications for healthcare and medicine.

"GOOGLE AI CHAIR" POSITION AT GOOGLE CLOUD, LEADING AI AND ML RESEARCH

The "Google AI Chair" position at Google Cloud is a leadership role focused on guiding and overseeing AI and machine learning (ML) research within the company. This position is critical in shaping the direction of AI technologies developed at Google.

REASON FOR ELECTION

Prior to her appointment, Fei-Fei Li was already a highly respected figure in AI research, particularly known for her work in computer vision and as a co-creator of the ImageNet project. Her academic and research background made her an ideal candidate for this high-impact role.
