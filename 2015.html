01

NATIONAL ACADEMY OF ENGINEERING (NAE)

The NAE is one of the highest professional honors accorded to an engineer in the United States. It recognizes individuals who have made outstanding contributions to engineering research, practice, or education.

REASON FOR ELECTION

Fei-Fei Li was recognized for her pioneering contributions to the field of computer vision, particularly for her work on large-scale image recognition and for her leadership in the development of ImageNet.

02

AMERICAN ASSOCIATION FOR THE ADVANCEMENT OF SCIENCE (AAAS)

Founded in 1848, AAAS is the world's largest general scientific society and publisher of the journal Science. The association seeks to "advance science, engineering, and innovation throughout the world for the benefit of all people."

REASON FOR ELECTION

Fei-Fei Li was recognized for her substantial contributions to the field of computer vision and artificial intelligence, particularly for her work in developing large-scale visual databases like ImageNet, which have enabled significant advancements in AI research.

03

ELECTED FELLOW OF THE ASSOCIATION FOR THE ADVANCEMENT OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE (AAAI)

AAAI is a professional organization dedicated to advancing the scientific understanding of artificial intelligence (AI). Founded in 1979, it promotes research and responsible usage of AI technologies, fostering collaboration among AI researchers and practitioners.

REASON FOR ELECTION

Fei-Fei Li was recognized for her groundbreaking contributions to computer vision and AI, particularly for her work on the ImageNet project and her influential research in visual recognition and understanding.
